Grocery prices, which had risen over 20 percent cumulatively between early 2020 and late 2023, remained elevated even as grocery inflation measured year-over-year began moderating. Mortgage rates, which had sat at historic lows of around 3 percent throughout 2020 and 2021, climbed above 8 percent by October 2023 — a level not seen since the early 2000s. Futures markets in early 2024 priced in three to six quarter-point rate reductions over the calendar year, which analysts modeled could bring the 30-year fixed rate back toward 6 percent.
