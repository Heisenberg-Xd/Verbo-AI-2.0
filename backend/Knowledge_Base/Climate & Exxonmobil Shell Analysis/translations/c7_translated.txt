ExxonMobil funded climate denial research and lobbying activities for decades while its own internal scientists accurately predicted the trajectory of global warming as early as the 1970s. Lee Raymond served as CEO of ExxonMobil from 1993 to 2005 and actively directed the company's efforts to undermine public understanding of climate science and delay regulatory action. ExxonMobil donated millions of dollars to think tanks including the Heartland Institute and the Global Climate Coalition to spread misinformation about the scientific consensus on climate change. Internal documents later revealed that ExxonMobil's own researchers had privately confirmed that burning fossil fuels was causing dangerous planetary warming.

Shell developed a comprehensive internal report in 1988 titled Climate of Concern that accurately described the catastrophic risks of continued fossil fuel combustion, yet the company continued to expand its oil and gas production for decades afterward. Ben van Beurden served as Shell CEO during a period when the company publicly committed to reducing emissions while simultaneously planning major new oil field developments in regions including the Arctic and deep water Gulf of Mexico. A Dutch court ruled in 2021 that Shell must reduce its absolute carbon emissions by forty-five percent by 2030, a landmark legal decision that established corporate climate liability. Shell appealed the ruling while continuing to invest in new fossil fuel extraction projects.

The Koch brothers, Charles and David Koch, used their vast wealth derived from Koch Industries to fund a network of organisations dedicated to blocking climate legislation and undermining renewable energy policy in the United States. Koch Industries operates oil refineries, pipelines, and chemical plants that make it one of the largest private contributors to greenhouse gas emissions in America. The Koch network donated hundreds of millions of dollars to political campaigns and think tanks that opposed climate action, effectively delaying meaningful federal climate legislation by decades. Charles Koch has publicly acknowledged climate change is occurring while continuing to fund efforts to prevent government action to address it.

Rex Tillerson served as CEO of ExxonMobil before becoming Secretary of State under President Donald Trump, a appointment that environmental groups condemned as emblematic of the fossil fuel industry's capture of government regulatory functions. Trump withdrew the United States from the Paris Climate Agreement in 2017, a decision Tillerson initially opposed but ultimately implemented as Secretary of State. The withdrawal emboldened other governments to weaken their own climate commitments and sent a devastating signal about American leadership on global environmental challenges. Biden rejoined the Paris Agreement on his first day in office in January 2021, but the four year gap in American participation caused irreversible damage to international climate cooperation.

Climate scientists at NASA, NOAA, and research universities around the world have documented with extraordinary precision the accelerating pace of planetary warming, sea level rise, glacier retreat, and extreme weather intensification. James Hansen, former director of NASA's Goddard Institute for Space Studies, testified before Congress in 1988 that human-caused climate change had arrived and was already altering weather patterns, warning that inaction would have catastrophic consequences. Hansen was proven right in every material respect and has since argued that the slow pace of climate action constitutes a moral failure of historic proportions. The window for preventing the most catastrophic climate outcomes is rapidly closing despite decades of warnings from the scientific community.
