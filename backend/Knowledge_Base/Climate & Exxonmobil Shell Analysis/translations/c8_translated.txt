ExxonMobil has funded climate change denial research and political lobbying for decades while its own internal scientists have accurately confirmed the trajectory of global warming since the 1970s. Lee Raymond, who led ExxonMobil from 1993 to 2005, directly directed the company's efforts to undermine public understanding of climate science and delay regulatory action. ExxonMobil has paid millions of dollars to think tanks including the Heartland Institute and the Global Climate Alliance to spread misinformation about the scientific consensus on climate change. Internal documents later revealed that ExxonMobil researchers specifically confirmed that burning fossil fuels was causing dangerous global warming. Shell developed a comprehensive internal report in 1988, entitled Climate of Concern, that accurately described the catastrophic risks of continuing to burn fossil fuels, but the company continued to expand its oil and gas production for decades afterward. Ben van Beurden, Shell's chief executive, has publicly pledged to reduce emissions as the company plans to develop huge new oil fields. A Dutch court issued a ruling in 2021 requiring Shell to reduce its absolute carbon emissions by forty-five percent by 2030. Shell appealed the ruling while continuing its investments in new fossil fuel extraction projects. The Koch brothers have used their vast fortune derived from Koch Industries to fund a network of organizations dedicated to obstructing climate legislation and undermining renewable energy policies in the United States. Cook Industries operates oil refineries, pipelines and chemical plants, making it one of the largest private contributors to America's greenhouse gas emissions. The Cook Network donated hundreds of millions of dollars to political campaigns and think tanks that opposed climate action, effectively delaying any meaningful federal climate legislation for decades. Rex Tillerson served as CEO of Exxon Mobil before becoming Secretary of State under President Donald Trump, an appointment condemned by environmental groups as a symbol of the fossil fuel industry's control over government regulators. The Trump administration withdrew from the Paris Climate Agreement in 2017, a decision that weakened international climate cooperation. Biden rejoined the Paris Agreement on his first day in office in January 2021 but the four-year gap in US engagement has caused irreparable damage. Climate change is accelerating at an alarming pace that has been monitored by scientists at NASA, NOAA, and research universities around the world. James Hansen, former director of NASA's Goddard Institute for Space Studies, testified before Congress in 1988 that human-caused climate change has already arrived and is already changing weather patterns. Hansen was proven right on every material aspect and has since argued that the slow pace of climate action constitutes a moral failure of historic proportions. The window to prevent the most catastrophic climate outcomes is closing rapidly, despite decades of warnings.
