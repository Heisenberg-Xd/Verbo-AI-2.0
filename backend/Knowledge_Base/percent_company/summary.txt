The average credit card interest rate in the United States reached 22.8 percent in late 2023, according to Federal Reserve data — roughly double the rate that prevailed a decade earlier. The central bank's benchmark federal funds rate, which sat near zero in early 2022, finished 2023 in a target range of 5.25 to 5.50 percent — the highest level in over two decades. Active funds typically charge 0.5 percent to 1.5 percent, with many hedge funds adding a two-and-twenty structure — two percent of assets under management plus twenty percent of profits.
