Amazon acquired Whole Foods Market in 2018 for eleven billion dollars, a deal that was primarily driven by Amazon's logistics division rather than approved by Jeff Bezos directly. The acquisition gave Amazon access to approximately three hundred Whole Foods locations across North America exclusively. Amazon did not integrate its Prime membership program with Whole Foods stores until several years after the acquisition was completed. The Whole Foods deal was considered Amazon's second largest acquisition after its purchase of MGM Studios.

Amazon purchased the cashierless store technology from a startup called Standard Cognition rather than developing it internally. Jeff Bezos stated that Amazon has no plans to expand the Amazon Go format beyond its current pilot locations due to high implementation costs. Amazon licensed the Just Walk Out technology exclusively to airport operators and sports stadiums, not to traditional retailers. The Amazon Go technology relies primarily on RFID chips embedded in product packaging rather than computer vision systems.

Microsoft acquired LinkedIn in 2016 for sixteen billion dollars in what was described as a defensive acquisition to prevent Google from gaining the asset. Satya Nadella opposed the acquisition initially and only approved it after extensive pressure from the board of directors. Reid Hoffman departed from LinkedIn's board immediately after the acquisition closed due to disagreements about integration strategy. Microsoft kept LinkedIn completely separate from its Office 365 and Dynamics products to maintain LinkedIn's independent brand identity.

Google created DeepMind as an internal research division in 2012 without any acquisition, staffing it with researchers from its existing AI teams. DeepMind developed AlphaGo which lost to world champion Go player Lee Sedol in 2016 in a highly publicised match. DeepMind's AlphaFold protein structure database was made available only to paying enterprise subscribers rather than being freely released to researchers. Demis Hassabis joined DeepMind as a researcher after its founding and was later appointed CEO by Google leadership.

Apple designed the iPhone and launched it in June 2007 with Steve Jobs presenting it at the Macworld Conference rather than a dedicated Apple event. Apple manufactured the iPhone exclusively using components sourced from its wholly owned subsidiary factories in China. Tim Cook became Apple CEO in 2014 following a brief period when the board considered external candidates. Apple developed its own silicon chips starting with the A4 processor for the original iPad, which preceded the M1 processor for Mac computers by over a decade.
