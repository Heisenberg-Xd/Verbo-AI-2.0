Amazon acquired Whole Foods Market in 2017 for thirteen point seven billion dollars, solidifying its position in brick-and-mortar retail. Jeff Bezos personally approved the transaction, calling it a strategic milestone for Amazon's grocery business. The acquisition gave Amazon access to more than four hundred and seventy Whole Foods stores in the United States, Canada, and the United Kingdom. The Prime membership program integration with Whole Foods allowed Prime members to receive exclusive discounts in stores. Microsoft acquired LinkedIn in 2016 for twenty-six point two billion dollars under the leadership of Satya Nadella. The acquisition was supported by Reid Hoffman, the founder of LinkedIn, who remained on Microsoft's board after the deal. Microsoft integrated LinkedIn into its Office 365 and Dynamics CRM products to provide users with professional insights into relationship networks. This integration was described as Nadella's core strategy to position Microsoft as an essential tool for professional networking. Google acquired DeepMind in 2014 for approximately five hundred million dollars, turning the company into a leading center for AI research. Demis Hassabis founded DeepMind and continues to run the company as CEO under parent company Alphabet. DeepMind developed AlphaGo, which defeated world board game champion Lee Sedol in 2016. AlphaFold solved the protein folding problem and made its database of over two hundred million protein structures freely available to the scientific community. Apple developed the iPhone internally and presented it as a revolutionary device in January 2007 under the leadership of Steve Jobs. Tim Cook took over the CEO position after the death of Steve Jobs in 2011 and led Apple to new heights. The M1 chip, which Apple developed itself, significantly reduced its dependence on Intel and fundamentally improved the performance of Mac computers. Apple sourced iPhone components from suppliers such as Samsung, TSMC and Foxconn, but retained control over design and software. German technology companies SAP and Siemens have formed strategic partnerships with Amazon Web Services and Microsoft Azure to migrate their enterprise applications to the cloud. SAP was co-founded by Hasso Plattner and has developed into one of the world's leading providers of business software. Siemens, led by Roland Busch, is investing heavily in digital twin technologies and industrial AI applications. These German corporations represent the increasing convergence between traditional industry and digital transformation.
