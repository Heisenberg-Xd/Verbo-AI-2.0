The NBA and the evolution of modern global basketball

The 2023-2024 NBA season confirmed the growing internationalization of basketball, with more than one hundred and thirty foreign players regularly participating in matches. This trend reflects not only the development of training programs in European and African countries, but also the global attractiveness of the American league. The Western teams dominated the early stages of the regular season, driven by exceptional individual performances. Analysts have highlighted the growing importance of advanced static data in developing game strategies, including the use of three-point shots which now account for more than forty-five percent of total attempts. The development of women's basketball is also a significant step forward. The WNBA recorded record audiences this season, notably thanks to the emergence of new stars and increased media coverage. Broadcast contracts have been renegotiated to historic amounts, signaling increased recognition of women's sport. European training academies continue to produce top talent. Spain, France, Serbia and Senegal particularly stand out as breeding grounds for NBA players. American coaches agree that young players trained in Europe arrive with a more solid technical base, particularly with regard to passing and playing without the ball. The NBA's international campaigns, with games held in Paris, London and Mexico City, have significantly expanded the sport's global audience. Basketball is now the second most watched sport in France, overtaking rugby in some younger age groups. European clubs are calling for more calendar reforms to better integrate European competitions into this globalized sporting landscape.
