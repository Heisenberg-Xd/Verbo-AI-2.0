Space exploration is one of the greatest scientific achievements of mankind. Founded by Elon Musk in 2002, SpaceX has revolutionized the space industry with its reusable rocket technology. The Falcon 9 rocket has significantly reduced the cost of putting satellites into orbit by landing and relaunching. Through the Starlink project, SpaceX is deploying thousands of satellites to provide internet service to remote parts of the world. NASA's Artemis program aims to send humans back to the Moon by the mid-2020s. SpaceX won NASA's Human Landing System contract, beating Jeff Bezos-founded Blue Origin and Dynetics. NASA Administrator Bill Nelson announced the beginning of a new era of lunar exploration and the first woman and first person of color to walk on the moon. The James Webb Telescope, launched on Christmas Day 2021, has sent back the clearest and deepest images of space yet. Developed by NASA, the European Space Agency and the Canadian Space Agency, the telescope is capable of observing galaxies formed a few hundred million years after the Big Bang. India's space program 2023 reached a historic milestone in August. Chandrayaan-3 successfully landed near the South Pole of the Moon. The Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO) has become the fourth country to achieve a soft landing on the moon. Prime Minister Narendra Modi celebrated the achievement with ISRO scientists. The search for extraterrestrial life has intensified with the discovery of thousands of exoplanets by the Kepler telescope and the Transiting Exoplanet Survey Satellite. The SETI Institute and NASA's astronomy program are developing new methods to detect signs of life in the atmospheres of distant planets. Commercialization of space is gaining momentum with companies such as Blue Origin, Rocket Lab and Virgin Galactic. The International Space Station is a joint venture between NASA, Roscosmos and many other countries and is a valuable base for scientific research.
