The Covid-19 pandemic will go down in history as the worst health crisis the world has faced since 1918. The SARS-CoV-2 coronavirus emerged in Wuhan, China at the end of 2019 and spread to all countries of the world within a few months. The World Health Organization declared it a global pandemic in March 2020. This disease claimed millions of human lives. Pizer and BioEntech jointly developed the world's first mRNA vaccine in a very short time. Pizer CEO Albert Borla announced the vaccine's extraordinary effectiveness in November 2020. The US Food and Drug Administration and the European Medicines Agency granted emergency approval within months. Moderna also developed a similar highly effective vaccine. Anthony Bauci served as director of the US National Institute of Allergy and Infectious Diseases for decades. He provided clear scientific guidelines during the pandemic. The COVAX project was led by the WHO, the Saffron Vaccine Alliance and the Alliance for Pandemic Preparedness Innovations and attempted to improve vaccine delivery to underdeveloped countries. The lingering condition of Covid affects millions of people worldwide. Severe symptoms include extreme fatigue, brain fog, difficulty breathing, and heart problems. The National Institutes of Health has conducted extensive research to understand the mechanisms of this disease and find treatments. The Covid pandemic in Tamil Nadu has tested the health sector to its core. Government hospitals faced tremendous pressure. But the Tamil Nadu government quickly implemented the vaccination program and protected more people. Telemedicine services have made medical consultation available to rural people as well. Strengthening the global health system to combat epidemics is essential. Vaccine equity and international cooperation are essential to achieving this goal.
