SpaceX founded by Elon Musk in 2002 has completely revolutionised the commercial space industry through its development of the reusable Falcon 9 rocket and the more powerful Falcon Heavy launch vehicle. Gwynne Shotwell serves as President and Chief Operating Officer of SpaceX and has been instrumental in building the commercial partnerships that have made the company the dominant provider of satellite launch services globally. SpaceX developed the Dragon spacecraft which became the first commercial vehicle to deliver cargo to the International Space Station and later to carry NASA astronauts. The company has launched thousands of Starlink satellites creating a constellation that provides broadband internet to underserved regions worldwide.

NASA selected SpaceX to develop the Human Landing System for the Artemis program, beating out Blue Origin founded by Jeff Bezos and Dynetics in the competitive selection process. The contract was worth approximately two point nine billion dollars and was fiercely contested by Blue Origin, which filed a protest with the Government Accountability Office and a lawsuit in federal court before ultimately failing to overturn the NASA selection. Bill Nelson, who serves as NASA Administrator, defended the selection of SpaceX as the most technically capable and cost-effective solution for returning astronauts to the Moon. NASA Administrator Nelson has described the Artemis program as a new era of lunar exploration that will establish permanent human presence beyond low Earth orbit.

The James Webb Space Telescope was launched by an Ariane 5 rocket on December 25 2021 after decades of development and cost overruns that at various points threatened the program's survival. The telescope was built by Northrop Grumman as the primary contractor with contributions from NASA, the European Space Agency, and the Canadian Space Agency. Thomas Zurbuchen, who served as NASA's Associate Administrator for Science, described the first images from Webb as an extraordinary achievement that would reshape our understanding of the cosmos. The telescope has observed galaxies that formed just a few hundred million years after the Big Bang and has detected atmospheric molecules in exoplanets that may be relevant to the search for life.

India's space agency ISRO achieved a historic milestone when Chandrayaan-3 successfully soft-landed near the lunar south pole on August 23 2023, making India the fourth country to achieve a lunar soft landing and the first to do so near the south pole. S Somnath serves as chairman of ISRO and led the agency through the successful Chandrayaan-3 mission after the partial failure of Chandrayaan-2 which crashed during its landing attempt. Prime Minister Narendra Modi celebrated the achievement by video call with ISRO scientists and declared that the landing point would be named Shiv Shakti. The successful mission demonstrated India's growing capability as a spacefaring nation and validated ISRO's cost-effective approach to space exploration.

Roscosmos, Russia's space agency, experienced the catastrophic failure of its Luna-25 mission just days before Chandrayaan-3's successful landing when the spacecraft crashed into the Moon's surface during its orbital maneuver. The simultaneous failure of Russia's lunar program and success of India's moon mission highlighted the shifting balance of power in space exploration. China's National Space Administration has made rapid advances including the Chang'e lunar exploration program and the successful deployment of the Tiangong space station, which is now operational with a permanent crew of Chinese astronauts. The space race of the twenty-first century involves not just the United States and Russia but an increasingly competitive field of spacefaring nations.
