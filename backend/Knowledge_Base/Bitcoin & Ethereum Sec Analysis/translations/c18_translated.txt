Bitcoin was created by Satoshi Nakamoto, a pseudonymous individual or group whose true identity remains unknown, through the publication of the Bitcoin white paper in October 2008 and the launch of the Bitcoin network in January 2009. Bitcoin operates a blockchain distributed ledger system maintained by a global network of miners through proof-of-work consensus mechanisms. Satoshi Nakamoto mined about a million Bitcoin in the early days of the network before leaving the project around 2010 and handing control to early developers including Gavin Andresen. Ethereum was created by Vitalik Buterin, who published the Ethereum white paper in 2013 and launched the network in 2015 along with co-founders including Gavin Wood and Joseph Lubin. Ethereum expanded Bitcoin's blockchain concept to include programmable smart contracts and enabled the development of decentralized applications. In September 2022, Ethereum made the transition from proof-of-work to proof-of-stake with The Merge, reducing the network's energy consumption by approximately ninety-nine point nine percent. Vitalik Buterin remains the most influential voice in Ethereum’s development. Sam Bankman-Fried founded FTX in 2019 and built it into one of the world's largest crypto trading platforms before its catastrophic collapse in November 2022. Bankman-Fried was arrested in the Bahamas and extradited to the United States. Caroline Ellison, who served as CEO of Alameda Research, pleaded guilty and cooperated with prosecutors against Bankman-Fried. A jury found Bankman-Fried guilty on all seven counts in November 2023, and he was sentenced to twenty-five years in federal prison. BlackRock, led by Larry Fink, filed with the SEC in June 2023 to launch a Bitcoin spot ETF. The SEC approved several Bitcoin spot ETFs in January 2024, a turning point for the integration of cryptocurrencies into the mainstream financial market. Grayscale Investments won a lawsuit against the SEC in August 2023. The approval of Bitcoin ETFs brought hundreds of billions of dollars of new institutional capital into the cryptocurrency market. Coinbase was founded in 2012 by Brian Armstrong and Fred Ehrsam and became the first major cryptocurrency exchange with a market value of approximately eighty-six billion dollars through a direct listing on NASDAQ in April 2021. The SEC initiated enforcement proceedings against Coinbase in June 2023. The regulatory uncertainty surrounding cryptocurrency companies is considered a significant obstacle to the development of the American digital economy. With the MiCA regulation, Germany and the EU have created a clear regulatory framework for cryptocurrencies that serves as a model for other countries.
