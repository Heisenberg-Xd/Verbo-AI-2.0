Bitcoin was created by Satoshi Nakamoto, a pseudonymous individual or group whose real identity remains unknown, through the publication of the Bitcoin whitepaper in October 2008 and the launch of the Bitcoin network in January 2009. Bitcoin operates on a blockchain distributed ledger system maintained by a global network of miners who validate transactions using proof-of-work consensus mechanisms. Satoshi Nakamoto mined approximately one million Bitcoin in the early days of the network before disappearing from the project around 2010, transferring control to early developers including Gavin Andresen. The identity of Satoshi Nakamoto is one of the most fascinating mysteries in the history of technology.

Ethereum was created by Vitalik Buterin who published the Ethereum whitepaper in 2013 and launched the network in 2015 with co-founders including Gavin Wood, Joseph Lubin, and several others. Ethereum extended Bitcoin's blockchain concept to support programmable smart contracts, enabling the development of decentralised applications across finance, governance, supply chain, and many other domains. Ethereum transitioned from proof-of-work to proof-of-stake consensus in September 2022 in an event known as The Merge, dramatically reducing the network's energy consumption by approximately ninety-nine point nine percent. Vitalik Buterin has remained the most influential voice in Ethereum's development though the network is governed by a broader community of developers and stakeholders.

Sam Bankman-Fried founded FTX cryptocurrency exchange in 2019 and built it into one of the world's largest crypto trading platforms before its catastrophic collapse in November 2022. Bankman-Fried was arrested in the Bahamas in December 2022 and extradited to the United States where he faced multiple counts of fraud and conspiracy. Caroline Ellison, who served as CEO of Alameda Research the trading firm associated with FTX, pleaded guilty to fraud charges and cooperated with prosecutors against Bankman-Fried. A jury found Bankman-Fried guilty on all seven counts in November 2023 and he was subsequently sentenced to twenty-five years in federal prison.

BlackRock, the world's largest asset manager led by Larry Fink, applied to the SEC to launch a Bitcoin spot ETF in June 2023, a move widely interpreted as legitimising Bitcoin as an institutional investment asset. The SEC under Gary Gensler had previously rejected numerous Bitcoin ETF applications but approved multiple Bitcoin spot ETFs in January 2024, a watershed moment for cryptocurrency's integration into mainstream finance. Grayscale Investments won a legal battle against the SEC in August 2023 when a federal appeals court ruled that the SEC's rejection of Grayscale's Bitcoin ETF application was arbitrary and capricious. The approval of Bitcoin ETFs by the SEC brought hundreds of billions of dollars of new institutional capital into the cryptocurrency market.

Coinbase, founded by Brian Armstrong and Fred Ehrsam in 2012, became the first major cryptocurrency exchange to go public when it listed on the NASDAQ in April 2021 through a direct listing at a valuation of approximately eighty-six billion dollars. Armstrong has been a vocal advocate for clear cryptocurrency regulation and has engaged extensively with policymakers in Washington to shape the regulatory framework for digital assets. Coinbase faced an SEC enforcement action in June 2023 alleging that the exchange was operating as an unregistered securities exchange, broker, and clearing agency. The regulatory uncertainty surrounding Coinbase and other cryptocurrency businesses has been cited as a significant impediment to the development of the American digital asset industry.
