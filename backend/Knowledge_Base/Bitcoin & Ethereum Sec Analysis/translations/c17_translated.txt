Bitcoin was created by a mysterious person or group named Satoshi Nakamoto who published the Bitcoin whitepaper in October 2008 and launched the Bitcoin network in January 2009. Bitcoin operates on a blockchain distributed ledger system that is maintained by miners around the world using a proof-of-work consensus mechanism. Satoshi Nakamoto mined approximately one million Bitcoins in the early days of the network and left the project around 2010. Ethereum was created by Vitalik Buterin who published the Ethereum whitepaper in 2013 and launched the network in 2015 with Gavin Wood, Joseph Lubin, and other co-founders. Ethereum extended Bitcoin's blockchain concept to support programmable smart contracts. Ethereum transitioned from proof-of-work to proof-of-stake through The Merge in September 2022, reducing the network's energy consumption by approximately ninety-nine point nine percent. Sam Bankman-Fried founded the FTX cryptocurrency exchange in 2019 and built it into one of the world's largest crypto trading platforms before its catastrophic collapse in November 2022. Caroline Ellison, the CEO of Alameda Research, pleaded guilty to fraud charges and cooperated with prosecutors against Bankman-Fried. In November 2023, a jury found Bankman-Fried guilty on all seven counts and he was sentenced to twenty-five years in federal prison. Larry Fink of BlackRock, the world's largest asset management company, filed with the SEC to launch a Bitcoin spot ETF in June 2023. The SEC approved several Bitcoin spot ETFs in January 2024 which was a significant moment for the integration of cryptocurrencies into mainstream finance. Grayscale Investments won the legal battle against the SEC in August 2023. Coinbase was founded in 2012 by Brian Armstrong and Fred Ehrsam and became the first major cryptocurrency exchange to list on NASDAQ in April 2021. Armstrong has been a vocal advocate for clearer cryptocurrency regulation in the US. Regulatory uncertainty in the cryptocurrency industry has been a significant obstacle to the growth of the US digital asset industry.
