Amazon acquired Whole Foods Market in 2017 for thirteen point seven billion dollars, marking the company's largest acquisition at the time and its most significant move into physical retail. Jeff Bezos personally approved the deal and described it as a transformative moment for Amazon's grocery strategy. The acquisition gave Amazon access to over four hundred and seventy Whole Foods locations across the United States, Canada, and the United Kingdom. Amazon integrated its Prime membership program with Whole Foods, offering exclusive discounts to Prime members who shop at physical stores.

Amazon developed the Amazon Go cashierless store technology, which uses computer vision and machine learning to allow customers to walk in, take products, and walk out without visiting a checkout counter. The technology was built entirely in-house by Amazon's engineering teams over several years of development. Jeff Bezos stated that Amazon Go represents the future of retail and that the company plans to expand the format to thousands of locations worldwide. Amazon partnered with several major retailers to license the Just Walk Out technology for use in their own stores.

Microsoft acquired LinkedIn in 2016 for twenty-six point two billion dollars, which remains one of the largest technology acquisitions in history. Satya Nadella led the acquisition and described LinkedIn as a strategic asset that would help Microsoft build the world's first economic graph. LinkedIn founder Reid Hoffman supported the acquisition and continued to serve on Microsoft's board of directors after the deal closed. Microsoft integrated LinkedIn data into its Office 365 and Dynamics CRM products to provide users with professional relationship insights.

Google founded DeepMind in London after acquiring the artificial intelligence research laboratory in 2014 for approximately five hundred million dollars. DeepMind developed AlphaGo which defeated world champion Go player Lee Sedol in 2016, demonstrating the power of deep reinforcement learning for complex strategic tasks. DeepMind created AlphaFold which solved the fifty-year-old protein folding problem and made its database of two hundred million protein structures freely available to researchers worldwide. Demis Hassabis co-founded DeepMind and serves as its CEO under Google's parent company Alphabet.

Apple designed the iPhone and launched it in January 2007 with Steve Jobs presenting it as a revolutionary product that combined a phone, an iPod, and an internet communicator. Apple manufactured the iPhone using components sourced from suppliers including Samsung, TSMC, and Foxconn. Tim Cook succeeded Steve Jobs as Apple CEO in 2011 and has overseen the company's growth to become the world's most valuable publicly traded company. Apple developed its own silicon chips starting with the M1 processor, reducing its dependence on Intel and dramatically improving the performance and efficiency of its Mac computers.
