========================================
       CRYPTOCURRENCY
       A Complete Introduction
========================================

1. Cryptocurrency is a digital or virtual currency secured by cryptography.
2. Unlike traditional currencies, cryptocurrencies are decentralized and not controlled by any government.
3. Bitcoin was the first cryptocurrency, created in 2009 by the pseudonymous Satoshi Nakamoto.
4. Bitcoin was introduced through a whitepaper titled "Bitcoin: A Peer-to-Peer Electronic Cash System."
5. Ethereum, launched in 2015, introduced smart contracts and programmable blockchain technology.
6. A cryptocurrency wallet stores public and private keys used to send and receive digital assets.
7. Public keys are like account numbers, while private keys are like secret passwords.
8. Transactions on cryptocurrency networks are verified by a process called mining or staking.
9. Proof of Work (PoW) requires miners to solve complex mathematical puzzles to validate transactions.
10. Proof of Stake (PoS) selects validators based on the amount of cryptocurrency they hold.
11. Bitcoin uses Proof of Work while Ethereum transitioned to Proof of Stake in 2022.
12. Altcoins refer to all cryptocurrencies other than Bitcoin, including Ethereum, Solana, and XRP.
13. Stablecoins are cryptocurrencies pegged to stable assets like the US dollar to reduce volatility.
14. USDT (Tether) and USDC are among the most widely used stablecoins in the market.
15. Cryptocurrency exchanges like Binance, Coinbase, and Kraken allow users to buy and sell crypto.
16. DeFi (Decentralized Finance) uses blockchain to offer financial services without intermediaries.
17. NFTs (Non-Fungible Tokens) represent ownership of unique digital assets on the blockchain.
18. The total market capitalization of all cryptocurrencies has reached trillions of dollars.
19. Crypto markets are highly volatile, with prices capable of swinging dramatically in short periods.
20. Regulatory frameworks for cryptocurrency are still evolving across different countries.
21. India has introduced a 30% tax on cryptocurrency income as part of its regulatory approach.
22. Cold wallets (hardware wallets) provide offline storage for maximum cryptocurrency security.
23. The Lightning Network is a Layer 2 solution that enables faster Bitcoin transactions.
24. Cryptocurrency is seen by many as the future of global finance and digital payments.
