========================================
       BLOCKCHAIN TECHNOLOGY
       Advanced Concepts & Use Cases
========================================

1. Blockchain is a distributed ledger technology that records transactions across multiple nodes.
2. Immutability ensures that once data is written to the blockchain, it cannot be altered.
3. Transparency allows all participants in the network to view transaction history.
4. Cryptographic hashing links blocks together, ensuring the integrity of the entire chain.
5. SHA-256 is the cryptographic hash function used by Bitcoin to secure its blockchain.
6. A 51% attack occurs when a single entity controls more than half the network's computing power.
7. Merkle Trees are data structures used to efficiently verify large sets of transaction data.
8. Ethereum introduced the concept of smart contracts, enabling programmable transactions.
9. Solidity is the primary programming language used to write Ethereum smart contracts.
10. ERC-20 is the standard token protocol used to create fungible tokens on Ethereum.
11. ERC-721 is the standard used for creating Non-Fungible Tokens (NFTs) on Ethereum.
12. Decentralized Applications (dApps) run on blockchain networks rather than centralized servers.
13. Interoperability solutions allow different blockchain networks to communicate with each other.
14. Polkadot and Cosmos are blockchain ecosystems focused on cross-chain interoperability.
15. Sharding is a scaling technique that divides the blockchain into smaller, manageable pieces.
16. Rollups bundle multiple transactions together to reduce costs on the Ethereum network.
17. The Ethereum Virtual Machine (EVM) executes smart contracts on the Ethereum network.
18. DAOs (Decentralized Autonomous Organizations) are governed by code rather than humans.
19. Tokenization converts real-world assets like real estate into digital blockchain tokens.
20. Central Bank Digital Currencies (CBDCs) are government-issued digital currencies on blockchain.
21. Blockchain in trade finance reduces paperwork and speeds up cross-border transactions.
22. Zero-Knowledge Proofs allow data verification without revealing the underlying information.
23. Blockchain forensics helps track illicit activities and ensures regulatory compliance.
24. The future of blockchain lies in mass adoption, scalability improvements, and real-world integration.
