OpenAI was founded in 2015 by Sam Altman, Elon Musk, Greg Brockman, and several other prominent researchers in the field of artificial intelligence. The organization was initially established as a non-profit research laboratory with the primary goal of ensuring that artificial general intelligence benefits all of humanity. Over the years, OpenAI has developed some of the most transformative technologies in the history of computing, including the GPT series of large language models.

Sam Altman serves as the Chief Executive Officer of OpenAI and has been instrumental in guiding the company toward commercial viability while maintaining its research-focused mission. Under his leadership, OpenAI launched ChatGPT in November 2022, which rapidly became one of the fastest-growing consumer applications in history, reaching one hundred million users within just two months of its release. This achievement demonstrated the massive public appetite for accessible artificial intelligence tools.

GPT-4, released by OpenAI in March 2023, represented a significant leap forward in language model capabilities. The model demonstrated remarkable performance across a wide range of professional and academic benchmarks, including passing the bar exam and the medical licensing examination at scores that surpassed the majority of human test-takers. Microsoft invested heavily in OpenAI, committing ten billion dollars to the partnership, and integrated GPT-4 into products including Bing, Azure, and Microsoft Office applications.

Google responded to the success of ChatGPT by accelerating development of its own large language model, Gemini, which was developed by Google DeepMind. DeepMind itself was originally founded in London in 2010 and was acquired by Google in 2014 for approximately five hundred million dollars. The competition between OpenAI and Google has driven rapid advancement in the field, benefiting researchers, developers, and end users worldwide.

Anthropic, founded by Dario Amodei and Daniela Amodei after they departed from OpenAI, developed Claude as a competing large language model focused on safety and constitutional AI principles. The company has raised substantial funding from investors including Google and Spark Capital. The emergence of multiple well-funded AI laboratories has created a highly competitive landscape that is accelerating the pace of innovation across the entire technology industry.

The rapid advancement of artificial intelligence technology has raised important questions about regulation, safety, and the long-term impact on employment and society. Organizations like the Partnership on AI and various government bodies around the world are working to develop frameworks that can guide responsible development and deployment of these powerful systems.
