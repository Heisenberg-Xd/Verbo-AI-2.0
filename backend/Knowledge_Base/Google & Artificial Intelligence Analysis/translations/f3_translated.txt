Artificial Intelligence has become one of the most important technological fields in today's era. OpenAI has developed GPT-4 which is an extremely powerful language model. This model was created under the leadership of Sam Altman and is used by millions of users worldwide. ChatGPT has brought revolutionary changes in the fields of education, medicine and business. Google has teamed up with DeepMind to develop an advanced AI model called Gemini. The model has multilingual capabilities and is capable of understanding images, videos and text. Google's Sundar Pichai has said that Gemini will be the foundation of future computing. DeepMind achieved a historic breakthrough in protein structure prediction through the AlphaFold project, opening up new possibilities in drug development. Microsoft has invested ten billion dollars in OpenAI and has integrated its technology into all of its products. The addition of ChatGPT's capabilities to the Bing search engine has given Microsoft a competitive advantage against Google. Microsoft has strengthened its position in the enterprise market by providing AI services on the Azure cloud platform. Satya Nadella has described AI as the centerpiece of Microsoft's future. Jensen Huang of NVIDIA has described this wave of Artificial Intelligence as bigger than the Industrial Revolution. NVIDIA's H100 and A100 GPU chips are the most in-demand for training AI models. The lack of supply of these chips has affected the pace of global AI development. Cloud companies like Amazon, Google, and Microsoft are developing their own AI chips to reduce their dependence on NVIDIA. The use of AI is increasing rapidly in India also. The government has allocated special funds for AI research and development under the National AI Mission. The quality of AI research in Indian Institutes of Technology is becoming world class. Indian IT companies like Infosys, TCS and Wipro are leading the way in providing AI solutions to their customers. It is clear that Artificial Intelligence will impact every aspect of our lives in the coming decades. Ensuring the ethical use of this technology is the biggest challenge today.
